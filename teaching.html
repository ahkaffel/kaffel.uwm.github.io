Academic Teaching Experience and Institutional Impact

Following his doctoral training and subsequent research appointments as a Research Associate Scientist at Brown University and the University of Maryland, Dr. Ahmed Kaffel developed an extensive and distinguished teaching career spanning over fifteen years across major research universities in the United States and internationally, including the University of Wisconsin–Milwaukee, Marquette University, Prince Sultan University, and Virginia Tech.

He has taught both foundational and advanced courses central to applied mathematics, computational science, and engineering education. His teaching is closely informed by his active research program in scientific computing, numerical simulation, fluid dynamics, biomedical modeling, and computational engineering, enabling students to engage directly with modern computational methods and interdisciplinary scientific applications.

Teaching Philosophy and Research-Integrated Instruction

Dr. Kaffel’s teaching philosophy emphasizes intellectual rigor, interdisciplinary integration, and research-informed instruction. He integrates advanced computational tools including MATLAB, Python, and scientific simulation methods, enabling students to connect mathematical theory with real-world scientific and engineering challenges.

He is fully prepared to teach and develop graduate-level courses in applied mathematics, scientific computing, and computational engineering, contributing to the advancement of graduate education and interdisciplinary research training.

Student Supervision, Mentorship, and Research Training

Dr. Kaffel has supervised and mentored undergraduate and graduate students in research projects involving scientific computing, numerical simulation, fluid dynamics, biomedical modeling, and computational engineering.

He has actively participated in competitive student research initiatives, including preparation and submission of research proposals supporting undergraduate research participation through programs such as the Summer Undergraduate Research Fellowship (SURF), helping train future scientists and engineers.

Academic Service and Professional Contributions

Dr. Kaffel has served as an elected member of academic award committees and actively contributes to academic service, grant proposal development, student research initiatives, and scholarly activities including journal publications, conference presentations, and book chapters.

He regularly participates in academic workshops and professional development activities, ensuring continuous integration of emerging scientific and computational advances into mathematics education.

Educational Mission

Dr. Kaffel’s educational mission is to train the next generation of scientists, engineers, and computational researchers through rigorous, modern, and interdisciplinary mathematical education that prepares students for leadership in academia, industry, and advanced research environments.
