Teaching and Mentorship

My teaching is grounded in innovation, interdisciplinarity, and inclusion. I design courses that build strong mathematical foundations while preparing students to solve real-world problems in engineering, science, data science, and computational modeling.

Teaching Philosophy

My passion for teaching mathematics has been a defining element of my academic career. Across diverse institutions in the United States and internationally, I have taught undergraduate and graduate courses, mentored students in research, and continually refined my approach to education. My teaching philosophy is anchored in innovation, interdisciplinarity, and inclusion.

I integrate modern computational tools such as MATLAB, Python, Mathematica, and Fortran into my courses, enabling students to develop practical computational skills alongside mathematical understanding. These tools empower students to engage with contemporary scientific and engineering challenges.

I emphasize conceptual understanding, critical thinking, and active learning. My courses connect mathematics to applications in engineering, biomedical systems, physics, and data science, enabling students to appreciate the power of mathematics in solving complex real-world problems.

Student Mentorship and Research Supervision

I have mentored undergraduate and graduate students in research projects involving multiscale modeling, numerical simulation, fluid mechanics, and medical imaging. Students under my supervision have developed advanced computational methods and gained valuable research experience.

My mentorship emphasizes developing students’ analytical thinking, computational skills, and confidence, preparing them for graduate study and careers in academia, industry, and engineering.

International Teaching Experience

I have taught students across multiple institutions including the University of Wisconsin–Milwaukee, Marquette University, Virginia Tech, Prince Sultan University, and the University of Tunis. These diverse teaching experiences have strengthened my ability to teach students from a wide range of academic and cultural backgrounds.

Commitment to Excellence in Education

My goal is to inspire students to see mathematics as a powerful tool for solving meaningful problems in science, engineering, medicine, and technology. I strive to create an inclusive, rigorous, and supportive learning environment that promotes intellectual growth and prepares students for success in research and professional careers.
