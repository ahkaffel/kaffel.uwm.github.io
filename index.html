Profile

I am an applied mathematician specializing in scientific computing, mathematical modeling, and computational simulation of complex physical and engineering systems. My work integrates rigorous mathematical analysis, advanced numerical methods, and high-performance computing to develop predictive models for multiphysics and multiscale phenomena.

I received my Ph.D. in Mathematics from Virginia Polytechnic Institute and State University (Virginia Tech), one of the leading institutions in applied and computational mathematics. My academic career includes research and faculty appointments at internationally recognized institutions, including Brown University, the University of Maryland, Penn State University, Marquette University, and the University of Wisconsin–Milwaukee.

My research addresses fundamental and applied challenges in fluid dynamics, porous media transport, multiphase systems, and scientific computing. I develop advanced mathematical and computational frameworks used in engineering, scientific research, and industrial applications.
