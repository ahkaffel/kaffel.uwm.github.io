Executive Profile

I am an applied mathematician and scientific computing specialist with extensive experience developing advanced mathematical models and computational methods for complex physical, engineering, and scientific systems. My work integrates rigorous mathematical analysis, high-performance numerical simulation, and modern computational frameworks to deliver predictive and scientifically reliable solutions across academic, industrial, and interdisciplinary environments.

I earned my Ph.D. in Mathematics from Virginia Polytechnic Institute and State University (Virginia Tech), one of the premier institutions in applied and computational mathematics. My academic and research career includes appointments at leading research institutions including Brown University, the University of Maryland, PSU University, Marquette University, and the University of Wisconsin–Milwaukee.

My expertise lies at the intersection of applied mathematics, computational science, fluid dynamics, and multiphysics modeling. I develop mathematical and computational frameworks that address fundamental scientific challenges and enable technological innovation in engineering, data science, and scientific computing.

Research Impact and Contributions

I have authored numerous peer-reviewed publications in internationally recognized journals including Physics of Fluids, International Journal of Multiphase Flow, and Transport in Porous Media. My research advances the mathematical and computational foundations of multiphase transport, interfacial dynamics, and multiscale physical systems.

My work contributes to scientific advancement across academic research, industrial engineering, and computational science, and supports the development of predictive modeling tools for complex physical and technological systems.

I am deeply committed to excellence in teaching, mentorship, and academic leadership.
